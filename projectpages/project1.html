INVISION PROTOTYPE

PROBLEM

Cloud storage users want a safe and reliable space to store their memories, work, music and more. There are many options out there, but users still face a number of frustrations. My research showed that users want better security measures, and a reliable system that won't fail them if their wifi goes down. Complicated organization, and saving links are among those frutrations and easy collaboration was also crucial to work teams.

SOLUTION

My research indicated a highly diverse audience that represented every kind of user. With extensive research the solution resulted in an app that inspired easy organization through folders and categories only. PUSH provides a secure offline version so users can work offline,and when it comes to our users feeling safe while sharing, they can depend on several layers of security. These features include expiration and a 6 digit custom code.

The
Product

After several rounds of in depth testing, interviews and iterations, I finally reached the final product that is clean, easy and aesthetically appealing that meets every users’ needs. PUSH was reborn throug co-creation with the participants and gave this product life!

Key
Features

• Categories & Folder options to inspire organization while storing items on the app.

• Layered security options: Expiration dates, passwords & 6 digit custom code

• Easily save links by pasting into a PUSH document or download the PUSH browser extension that will allow an easy right click and “save to PUSH”

• Real time collaboration on tasks and events

• Offline system version

Competative
Analysis

Where would PUSH enter and stand on the market against other powerhouse storage systems that already exist? In the competitive analysis, I compared the apps which participants currently use for cloud storage and organization.

The competative analysis assisted me in finding the opportunities to take when compared to other big name storage products. While creating PUSH, and focusing on functionality and the experience our users needed, I also decided that this product would offer:

Compatible across all operating systems

Provide faster speeds

Digital codes for every shared file along with notifications of when your file is being viewed and by who.

Information Architeture

Sketching &
Wireframes

I started with sketches and cycled through iteratations after several testing sessions with paper prototypes. I moved onto creating wireframes in Figma and then put them to work by testing several times with participants. On the low fidelity wireframes, some particpants found that navigating was easy and understood the product’s purpose. I moved on to creating hi-fi wireframes and the feedback indicated that the naviagtion system was not as clear, participants were confused about whether the left navigation were links or buttons.

Iterations

Color and typography were added and I created a clickable prototype with invision and tested again. The participants were excited by the visuals but it soon proved to be overstimulating. The gradients looked cool but it caused confusion and distraction and the navigation was not a solid system, particularly for the homepage. Participants had a very hard time figuring out how to navigate. With this feedback I was forced to take a pause for the cause! I stepped back from the project for two days, and after clearing my mind, I redesgned everything.

Usability
Testing

Testing was done remotely and in-person. Using clickable prototypes and retesting several times would ensure the design was user-friendly.

After I conducted the usability tests for the prototype, there were still a few things that needed to be improved to ensure a seamless experience. Learn more about the users here.

Findings

• Navigation still needed a bit of work. The users navigated easily although the location of the navigation paired with the encasing on a rectangular shape looks as if they could be buttons or links.

• Shorten the length of the menu. When the “+” symbol is activated to create, upload etc. hesitation with the users was present. I needed to elimante that feeling of uncertainty.

• Add another “+” symbol in the list of files section and designate that as a create a new folder or category for organizing.

• The sign-up page was confusing for the first few users. They were confused about whether they should fill in the fields and then select the plans -OR- review and select the plans and move onto completing the information fields.

• Create icons that are complimentary sizes for sizes for the label buttons,previews of folders, files, and categories.

UI & Branding

Branding

The name PUSH was inspired by some of the participants. I asked three simple questions to provoke more freedom during their thought process. insteadof overwhelming them with a ton of questions, I wanted to focus on the end goal which is how they feel or how they connect to the product they are using. What do you think of when using a cloud storage? What do you think of when it comes to WHY you are using the cloud storage app? What emotion do you associate with it?

PUSH represents the motivation, confidence and connection that users want to achieve and experience while using this product. It also represents the literal pushing of files, documents, links, videos etc into the cloud. From there, things were moving on up and my product started to take shape,but not without trial and error.

Logo
Design

Emphasis was placed on the simple word “push” by using all capitals. From there I incorporated the upload symbol that the world is so familiar with, into the “H”. Users found it creative and amusing.

Color &
Typography

A clean appearance was achieved by using a medium blue, grays and a splash of accent color, yellow, allowing the users to feel calm, less stressed and confident while using this product.

With the concept of “Pushing files”, it was necessary to use a font that was tech related, Share Tech was perfect and paired with an easy to read and beautiful typeface, Montserrat. Share Tech is used for headlines and Montserrat, for the body. The combination engages the users and allows them to find and understand the information efficiently. What I
Learned

Through my design process I found that overstimulating users is definitely possible. Distractions like the over stimulating aspects of product, resulted in a poor experience. I became overly concerned with trends, and visuals that I did not have much experience using, only to crash and burn. After revisiting my surveys,analysis and performing more testing I came back to what the goal actually was, and what solution was needed. I revamped the entire product from some of the major wireframes to the icons, buttons and color palette. The users instantly felt the growth of the project and dove right in without hesitation, it was as if they used the product before. It was clean, easy and aesthetically appealing. I constantly called, chatted and asked questions and requested explanations. My biggest accomplishment is when I realized how much value there is in allowing the users to co-create.
