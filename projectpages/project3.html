VISIT WEBSITE

Why Re-Design?

My client needed to update her current Life Counseling website. The website was not user-friendly and did not convey the message she wished to communicate to her current and future clientele. As an accomplished Coach she wanted a website that met her clients needs and was also up to speed on trends.

Solution

Using the client’s desired web builder, I created a solution that was not only visually appealing but it was simple and, most importantly user-friendly and inviting. The navigation system and hierarchy of the entire site has been improved for better readability, usability and an improved user experience for individuals seeking these services.

Challenges

Using website builders can be very tedius and limiting when creating a custom site. There were many frustrations due to limitations. Though there was an option to write custom code, they limited to inline styling which is not best practices for clean coding.

A/B
Testing

During the process the client wanted to go back to a similar color theme that was originally on the site. We originially had changed the theme from a wood like background and red to a clean palette of blue, white and grey, which is popular in professional settings. The only difference here was that we kept the white background and changed all of the blue to a warmer red. Early stages they both performed equally, though over a few hours and 22 of participants later, it was clear that the BLUE theme was better.

Landing
Page

The landing page is now a beautiful, bright and functional design. I decided to go with a color scheme that tested well with users and the client agreed. It also helped that certain color schemes are traditional to certain industries, for example blue and green are closely associated with medical fields. After pairing a few fonts together, I chose Sorts Mills Goudy and Open Sans. Sorts Mills Goudy is an excellent candidate for subtle beaut in headlines and course Open Sans for smoothe readability.The imagery was enhanced with more fitting and higher quality images. I focused on images that were uplifting, bright and relayed a message of being supported. My client also wanted to have some type of "movement" on her new website, so I created an automated slide show with the images.
