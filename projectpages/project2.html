MARVEL PROTOTYPE

Problem

Herbal teas have been a big trend for the last few years and we see new ones hitting the shelves all the time. My client needed to solidify and requested an e-commerce store design to shed light on the possibilities of promoting and growing his product and brand. Health Hooch is a homemade herbal tonic with major health benefits The goal was to provide a user-friendly, aesthetically appealing online store, and an easy shopping experience where consumers can shop for the product but also gain knowledge about the ingredients and benefits. The functional, clean design as well as strong brand development will be introduced to investors, and manufactures. My client also plans to apply the design to his designated e-commerce platform.

Solution

After going over details of the project and having in-depth conversation about the brand I was able to provide a user-friendly, bright and organized online store design. I also developed a brand that sends a message of strength and health. Health Hooch not only provides organic and safe products to consumers but they also learn while they're shopping. The store gives users an option to subscribe to a monthly plan or shop as guests giving them more purchasing power and freedom, which we believe will result in loyal subscribers.

Visual Design

Branding

Branding was very interesting! I had the chance to really dive into the history of Hooch. I could see where the product stood out compared to it's competitors. Hooch is originally a potent alcoholic beverage usually made in the woods because it is highly illegal. Names that most people are familiar with are: Moonshine, white lightning, mountain dew, choop, homebrew, shiney, or white whiskey. With that in mind, we knew that the route we wanted to take Health Hooch was a rugged, dark and natural theme.

Logo
Design

I performed market research on 4 other logo designs from our competitors and incorporated things that my client liked from each. This was strictly experimental and assisted in solidifying the path for Health Hooch. I worked down to 3 final sketches and placed them on packaging mockups for my client to review as well. The current logo name is enclosed inside large typeface of the same letter, simple but creative, this worked well for marketing and trending names like "HH". A ring was added, which represents the bottom of a mason jar, which hooch is closely associated with. To ensure our users understood that this is a healthy product I added custom vector leaves at the bottom of the ring.

Color &
Typography

The color palette was fairly easy to accomplish, although there was hesitation on different shades of each color selected. We wanted to ensure that we were capturing the boldness of Health Hooch through it's colors. Nature colors were selected on a darker scale and I also aimed for modern fitness colors. I chose Oswald for it's origin in classic gothic and grotesque styles of the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries. Oswald is available in light, normal and bold weights. I felt that this typeface fit perfectly with the bold brand created for Health Hooch. I decided to pair it with an easy to read and versatile typeface, Roboto , used only for body context.

A/B
Testing

Version 1 of the Health Hooch landing page represented the first thoughts of a rugged, warm and healthy drink, though it was much too darkerand didn't have a "store" vibe. Version 2 represents a more modern, e-commerce yet health brand that I was aiming for. I tested both versions and Version 2 was much more popular due to clean and modern appearance and clear pathway to navigate throughout the site.
